# If Influence Is So Powerful, Why Isn't It Our Default?

## 1. The hook — the uncomfortable question
- Everyone in marketing/startups has "read" *Influence*. It's 30+ years old. The principles are famous.
- So why, when we sit down to convert a trial or close a deal, do we still reach for the discount code, the 40-logo wall, the mandate?
- If they're so powerful, why aren't they the *default*?

## 2. Because the default is easy and legible — the right move isn't
- The mistake is always the low-effort, measurable action: discount %, logo count, "just send the email."
- The Cialdini move is effortful and often invisible to a dashboard: *gave value first*, *let the team co-create*, *earned liking before pitching*. You can't put "reciprocity" in a weekly metrics review.
- Orgs optimize what they can see. The default wins by inertia.

## 3. Because we know the names, not the mechanisms
- "Social proof" → so we add *more* logos. But the lever is **similarity**, not volume; irrelevant proof can *lower* a matching buyer's confidence.
- Familiarity breeds confident misapplication → it underperforms → we conclude "this stuff doesn't really work." (It does; we did it wrong.)

## 4. Because the good moves require swallowing short-term risk
- Admit a weakness. Cap enrollment. Give before asking. Kill a reliable weekly sale.
- These pay off on long horizons; quarterly targets reward the transactional default. Incentives quietly punish the better move.

## 5. Because the tactics got cargo-culted into dark patterns
- Fake countdown timers, "Only 1 left!" that resets on refresh. People copied the *surface*, not the principle.
- Detected → backfires → trust craters → the honest versions get lumped in with the scams. (→ segue to reproducibility)

> **Sidebar — the dark pattern that became a legal liability.**
> Booking.com's "Only 1 room left!" drew UK **CMA** enforcement, an EU order to
> strip the tactics, and a 2025 consumer claim backed by 130,000+ people, with
> damages estimated over €1bn. Manufactured urgency, *detected*, doesn't just
> underperform — it backfires. (Browse the Deceptive Design "Hall of Shame" for
> the full rogues' gallery.)

## 6. An honest note on the reproducibility problem
- The replication crisis hit social psychology hard. Priming / embodied-cognition-style
  effects (Bargh-adjacent) shrank or vanished on re-testing; marketers love to over-cite
  the flashy lab number.
- But not all of it is shaky: reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social-proof-via-similarity,
  and honest scarcity have decent *field* support, not just lab priming.
- The move: **know the principle, distrust the specific demo, and test it on your own
  audience.** Treat the shaky ones as hypotheses, not laws.

## 7. What to actually do today (for founders / product builders)

| If you're trying to… | ❌ The default | ✓ Do this instead | Principle | Go look |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Get noticed by cold prospects | Gate a generic ebook, then pitch | Give something genuinely useful *first* | Reciprocity | HubSpot's free Website Grader (4M+ sites graded) |
| Grow by word-of-mouth | Bolt on a cash bounty buried in settings | Reward *both sides* with more of the product, at high-intent moments | Reciprocity + Social proof | Dropbox — 500MB each way, ~3,900% growth in 15 months |
| Build a retention habit | Nag with "come back!" emails | Turn the big goal into a daily streak + a *commitment* ("Commit to my goal") | Commitment & Consistency | Duolingo — streaks lifted Day-7 retention +14% |
| Launch a new product | Open the floodgates with a banner — or fake a countdown | *Earned* scarcity tied to a real operational reason | Scarcity (honest) | Superhuman — 180K-person waitlist + 1:1 onboarding |
| Make your proof convert | A 40-logo "trusted by" wall | 3–5 case studies from customers who look *exactly* like the buyer | Social proof = similarity, not volume | _NOTE: pick example — Gong/Stripe segmented case studies?_ |
| Earn trust / hire | Guarded, polished PR | Publish the uncomfortable numbers | Authority via candor | Buffer — open salaries + live revenue → 229% more applicants |
| Reduce churn | Fire off a win-back discount | Make users *co-creators*, then credit them publicly | Unity | Figma — 1,500+ community plugins drive ~50% of engagement |
| Learn what customers want | "Would you use this?" / pitch the idea | "Walk me through the *last time* you hit this problem" — don't pitch | Behavior > politeness | The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick) |

## 8. Close
- The principles aren't secret and they aren't the problem. The problem is that the lazy
  default is *right there*, and the better move costs effort, patience, and a little ego.
- Pick the durable few. Use them on purpose. Test the rest. That's the whole edge.
